# When to Replace This Page with Formal Legal Review

These policies have been written to be safe, honest, and professional for a pre-launch SaaS platform. However, they are **not a substitute for formal legal review**.

## Replace or formally review these policies when:

- **Before public launch or accepting paying customers**
  - Engage a lawyer specialising in Australian privacy law and SaaS agreements
  - Have all policies reviewed for compliance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth)
  - Ensure terms adequately protect CertSync from liability

- **Before processing sensitive workplace data at scale**
  - When handling high-risk work licences, WorkSafe credentials, or contractor compliance data
  - Before signing enterprise contracts with construction companies, mining contractors, or labour hire firms
  - When organisations request data processing agreements (DPAs)

- **If you expand beyond Australia**
  - GDPR compliance required for European users
  - CCPA/CPRA compliance for California residents
  - Additional jurisdictional requirements may apply

- **Before pursuing security certifications**
  - SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, or other compliance frameworks require formal policy documentation
  - Certification auditors will require professionally drafted security and privacy policies
  - Legal review ensures policies align with certification requirements

- **If handling payment data directly**
  - PCI-DSS compliance requires specific security policies
  - Additional liability protections needed for financial transactions

- **When raising investment or selling the company**
  - Investors and acquirers will conduct legal due diligence
  - Professional legal documentation demonstrates maturity and reduces risk

- **If you receive a legal complaint, data breach, or regulatory inquiry**
  - Immediate legal counsel required
  - Policies may need to be updated to reflect incident response procedures

- **When user base exceeds 1,000 active users**
  - Increased liability exposure warrants professional legal protection
  - Enterprise customers may request customised terms or DPAs

## What a lawyer will help with:

✅ Ensure compliance with Australian Privacy Principles (APPs)
✅ Clarify liability limitations and indemnification clauses
✅ Draft enforceable terms that protect CertSync's intellectual property
✅ Create jurisdiction-specific policies (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)
✅ Review insurance coverage and risk exposure
✅ Draft data processing agreements (DPAs) for enterprise customers
✅ Ensure terms align with industry standards and best practices

## Recommended next steps:

1. **Budget for legal review before launch** (~$3,000–$8,000 AUD for initial policies)
2. **Engage a lawyer experienced in Australian SaaS and privacy law**
3. **Consider ongoing legal retainer** for policy updates, customer contracts, and compliance questions
4. **Review policies annually** or after any significant product changes

## In the meantime:

These policies are:
- ✅ Honest about what CertSync does and doesn't do
- ✅ Transparent about early-stage status
- ✅ Free from false compliance claims
- ✅ Safe for pre-launch testing and waitlist signups

They are **NOT**:
- ❌ A substitute for legal advice
- ❌ Suitable for high-risk commercial contracts
- ❌ Adequate for international expansion
- ❌ Defensible in complex legal disputes

---

**When in doubt, get legal advice before making claims about security, compliance, or data protection.**
