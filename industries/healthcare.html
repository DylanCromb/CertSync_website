Healthcare workforce records

Healthcare credential tracking gets messy across roles, checks and renewals

Healthcare, aged care, disability and community service teams often manage registrations, CPR, first aid, mandatory training, screening checks, onboarding evidence and role-specific requirements. When records are scattered across folders and spreadsheets, it is harder to see what is current, missing or expiring.

CertSync gives workforce, operations and admin teams one register for the records they already manage, without replacing clinical governance, workforce policy, legal advice or regulator guidance.

Healthcare credential tracking FAQs

What is healthcare credential tracking software?

Healthcare credential tracking software helps teams organise professional registrations, training certificates, CPR, first aid, screening checks, onboarding evidence and renewal dates in one workforce register.

Can CertSync track healthcare training and registrations?

CertSync can track registrations, first aid, CPR, mandatory training, screening checks and other healthcare workforce credentials with evidence files and expiry dates.

Can CertSync track AHPRA registration evidence?

Yes. CertSync can store AHPRA registration evidence and renewal dates where your team chooses to manage those records.

Can CertSync track screening checks?

Yes. CertSync can help teams store and track evidence for police checks, Working with Children Checks, NDIS Worker Screening and other checks where applicable to their workforce.

Can CertSync send healthcare credential expiry reminders?

Yes. CertSync can help teams monitor expiry dates and send reminders before registrations, training certificates, CPR, first aid and screening records lapse.

Can we export healthcare workforce evidence?

Yes. CertSync helps teams export records by worker, role, team, document type, status or expiry window for audits, internal reviews and manager visibility.

Is CertSync a clinical compliance adviser?

No. CertSync provides credential record management, expiry tracking and reporting tools. Teams should confirm their own regulatory, workforce and clinical governance requirements.
