What is CertSync?

Compliance precedents (public regulator actions)

SWMS not provided before high-risk work commenced

A Victorian court imposed a $850,000 fine after finding that critical WHS documentation was not provided to workers before high-risk construction work began, culminating in a fatal workplace fall.

The outcome shows why high-risk teams need a clear way to manage required documents and keep evidence easier to find before work starts.

Read the WorkSafe Victoria prosecution outcome

Building work proceeded without licence verification

A Tasmanian court imposed a $40,000 penalty after finding that building and roof plumbing work was carried out without the required licences being held or verified before work commenced.

The case illustrates the risk of relying on fragmented licence records, emails and manual checks when work is being planned.

Read the government court outcome

Work performed without registration verification

AHPRA successfully prosecuted an individual for misrepresenting professional registration status, with the court imposing $60,000 in fines plus costs.

In regulated health services, registration evidence needs to be easy to review. The case shows why current records and expiry dates should not be buried in inboxes or shared folders.
