How to Keep Contractor Evidence Ready for Audits and Client Requests

Businesses can keep contractor evidence ready by storing required documents in one register, tracking expiry dates and status, and making records easier to export or produce when requested. This supports audit, builder, insurer, client and internal review workflows without claiming to guarantee audit outcomes.

Why evidence readiness matters

Evidence requests often arrive when a team is already busy. Builders, principal contractors, insurers, auditors and internal managers may ask for licences, insurances, SWMS, inductions, permits or training records with little warning.

When records are organised before the request arrives, the team spends less time rebuilding folders and more time responding with the stored evidence it already manages.

Evidence commonly requested

  • Contractor licences and company records
  • Insurance certificates and renewal dates
  • SWMS, permits and site documents
  • Worker inductions, training records and tickets

Keep files and status together

Evidence is easier to review when the file, expiry date, owner and status are attached to the same record.

Check expiry before the request

Expired insurance, licences or training records are easier to follow up when they are surfaced before a review.

Export stored records faster

Exporting from a register is usually faster than rebuilding evidence packs from inboxes and shared folders.

Where CertSync fits
