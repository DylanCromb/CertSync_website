Best Way to Track Employee Licence Expiry Dates

The best way to track employee licence expiry dates is to keep each worker, credential type, evidence file, expiry date, status and reminder schedule in one central register. Spreadsheets can work early on, but they become harder to manage when a business has multiple workers, contractors, document types and renewal dates.

Why licence expiry tracking matters

Expired licences, tickets and permits can slow down rostering, site access, client approvals and internal reviews. The risk is usually not that a team has no information. It is that the file, expiry date, worker owner and renewal reminder are split across different places.

A better workflow keeps records, status and reminders together so managers can see what needs attention before a licence or certificate lapses.

What to capture

  • Worker name, role and team
  • Document type and evidence file
  • Issue date, expiry date and renewal owner
  • Status, review notes and reminder schedule

Why spreadsheets become risky

Spreadsheets separate the file from the reminder. Unless someone checks them constantly, expired or missing records can be overlooked.

What a better system should include

Look for worker profiles, document types, expiry dates, evidence storage, status fields and reminders that surface upcoming renewals.
